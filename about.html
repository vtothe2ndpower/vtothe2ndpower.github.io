BIO

In late 2019 I heard the following quote from motivational speaker Brian Tracy: "If you do not love your job then get out of it like you would get out of a burning house." At the time I was working as a Credit Analyst and feeling a bit unfulfilled. What bothered me about this role was that I had an Applied Mathematics degree from UC Berkeley which I wasn't even using. Funny enough, I'd came across a Reddit post around the same time written by someone who'd taught themselves how to code over the span of 6 months and how it changed their life completely. From then on I became more and more curious about this 'code thing' and began dabbling with computer programming during my lunch breaks and free time. It didn't take long before I decided to leave this job and teach myself how to code full-time. I was committed to the goal and ready to see it through. My undergraduate studies in math naturally attracted me to the problem solving within software engineering. I loved most that I was challenged constantly, forced to get myself unstuck when faced with complex problems, and that there was always something new to learn next.
